## What's happening with YOUR pension

In May 2025, the Arkansas Public Employees' Retirement System (APERS) Investment Subcommittee authorized **$25–50 million** in Israel Bonds purchases — direct loans to the Israeli government that cannot be sold before maturity.

This authorization came after all three major credit agencies downgraded Israel's credit rating.

## Why it matters

- **Your retirement security is at stake.** APERS manages pensions for tens of thousands of Arkansas state employees and retirees.
- **Credit risk is real.** All three major rating agencies have downgraded Israel's credit, increasing the risk of these investments.
- **No independent analysis was performed.** Standard fiduciary practice requires pension funds to conduct their own due diligence before committing member funds.
