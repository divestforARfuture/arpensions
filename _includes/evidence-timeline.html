2017
State Auditor Dennis Milligan, after being approached by the bond issuer's sales representatives, reached out to then-Senator Jason Rapert to sponsor legislation enabling the state to purchase non-tradable sovereign bonds
Oct. 2023
State Board of Finance approves $10 million additional purchase of non-tradable sovereign bonds, bringing total holdings to $57 million
Late 2024
Senior Treasury investment manager writes internal memo recommending against new sovereign bond purchases, citing credit downgrades from three major rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, and Fitch)
Nov. 2024
Auditor Milligan arranges meeting between the issuer's representatives and state officials
Key escalation: April 2025
The bond issuer's sales representatives meet with directors of ATRS, APERS, the State Treasurer, and the State Auditor
Key escalation: May 2025
Treasury purchases $20 million in new non-tradable sovereign bonds; APERS Investment Subcommittee authorizes $25–50 million
Key escalation: June 2, 2025
ATRS Board adopts Resolution 2025-22 authorizing up to $50 million; Board Chair Danny Knight casts lone “no” vote
July 2025
Arkansas Times publishes in-depth investigation documenting the timeline, political connections, and fiduciary concerns
Aug. 2025
Governor Sanders publicly celebrates the state's sovereign bond investments during a foreign trade mission
