## Our mission

Divest for AR Future is a grassroots campaign committed to exposing and challenging the misuse of public funds, holding decision-makers accountable to their fiduciary duties, and building a broad-based movement to align Arkansas's investments with the values of transparency, justice, and the well-being of Arkansans.

## Our approach

We believe in evidence-based advocacy. Our campaign is built on:

- **Public records research** — analyzing 957 documents obtained through FOIA requests to four Arkansas state agencies
- **Fiduciary-first framing** — every claim we make is rooted in financial analysis and legal standards, not political arguments
- **Coalition building** — working with educators, public employees, and community organizations across Arkansas

All claims on this website are backed by public records obtained through lawful FOIA requests.

## Frequently asked questions

**Isn't this about politics?**
No. This is about fiduciary duty and financial risk. Arkansas law requires that pension investments be made based on "pecuniary factors" — financial merit. We're asking whether that standard was met.

**What are you asking for?**
Three things: (1) a pause on new Israel Bonds purchases until an independent credit analysis is completed; (2) transparent reporting on current holdings and their performance; (3) a review of the process that led to recent authorizations.

**Is this legal?**
Absolutely. FOIA requests are a fundamental right under Arkansas law. Asking elected officials and appointed trustees to account for their investment decisions is exactly what pension oversight laws are designed to enable.
